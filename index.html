How to play Outspell online

1

Place your first word

Drag and drop tiles from your rack onto the board to spell a word. Your first word must pass through the center star.

2

Use bonus cells and exchange tiles

Use bonus cells to earn more points: blue = letter bonus; red = word bonus.

If you can't find a word, use Exchange to swap tiles. This skips your turn but can reset a bad rack.

3

Connect and extend words

Every new word must contain at least one letter that's already on the board. Place tiles along a single line horizontally or vertically. Maximize your score by adding letters to existing words and spelling new words.

Tips

  • Multipliers: Stretch to premium cells even if the base word is short.
  • Rack balance: Keep a mix of vowels and consonants; exchange when stuck.
  • Prefixes/suffixes: Add -ED, -ER, RE-, UN-, and common hooks to extend words.
  • Defense: Avoid opening triple-word lines unless you can control the reply.
  • End game: Track remaining tiles and set up high-value closures.

Outspell Game Rules

  • Form valid words horizontally or vertically using tiles from your rack.
  • Your first word must pass through the center star.
  • Letter and word multipliers apply only on the turn a tile is first placed.
  • All new words formed in a placement must be valid.
  • Use all 7 tiles in a single move to earn a bingo bonus.
  • You may exchange tiles or pass your turn when available.

FAQs about Outspell Game

What is Outspell?

Outspell is a browser-based word strategy game. Form words on a board, stack multipliers, and use all 7 tiles for a bingo bonus. Quick to learn, deep to master.

How do you play Outspell Game?

  1. Start: Click Play to start a solo match and draw 7 tiles. First word must cross the center.
  2. Place: Drag tiles to build valid words horizontally or vertically.
  3. Score: Target multiplier cells and set up parallel placements for combo points.

How does outspell game scoring work?

  • Letters + multipliers: Red cells boost word scores; blue boost letter values.
  • Bingo bonus: Use all 7 tiles in one move for a big bonus.
  • Parallel words: Overlaps can score multiple words in one placement.

How to get more points on Outspell?

To maximize your points in Outspell, focus on these strategic approaches:

  • Target multiplier squares: Place high-value letters on double/triple letter scores, and position entire words on double/triple word scores
  • Create parallel words: Build words that run parallel to existing ones to score multiple words simultaneously
  • Save premium letters: Hold onto Q, X, Z, and J for high-scoring opportunities
  • Go for bingos: Using all 7 tiles in one turn gives you a substantial bonus
  • Plan ahead: Set up your rack for future high-scoring plays by keeping balanced vowels and consonants
  • Use short connecting words: Two-letter words like "QI" or "XI" can help you place premium letters on multiplier squares

Is "ee" an Outspell word?

No, "ee" is not a valid word in Outspell. The game uses a comprehensive English dictionary that excludes most two-letter combinations that aren't recognized words. Valid two-letter words include common ones like "is," "it," "an," "or," "we," and "go." For strategic play, familiarize yourself with accepted two-letter words as they're crucial for connecting longer words and accessing premium squares.

Is "za" a word in Outspell?

Yes, "za" is a valid word in Outspell! It's slang for "pizza" and is accepted in most standard word game dictionaries. This makes it particularly valuable because the Z tile is worth 10 points, so "za" can be a high-scoring two-letter word. Other useful Z words to remember include "zi" (a type of energy in Chinese philosophy) and "zo" (a Tibetan hybrid animal). These short Z words are excellent for strategic placement on premium squares.
